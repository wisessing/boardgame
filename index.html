Hex tile board · polyhex gallery

Each tile is a hex with a center node and 1–6 edges to its side midpoints. For each size N, every connected arrangement of N hex cells in which every cell shares an edge with at least three others (free polyhex, deduped under D6 symmetry) is rendered with all 6 edges active. Polyhexes are grouped by the abstract graph they induce — vertices = tile centers, edges = adjacency between tiles. The rule is restrictive: the smallest non-empty case is N = 7 (the centered hex flower).
